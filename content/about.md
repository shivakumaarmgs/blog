## Background

I'm a senior engineer and team lead based in Chennai with **12+ years** of experience across the full arc of product engineering — from shipping client apps to owning platforms to running cross-functional teams in regulated fintech. Currently at Solaris Group, where I lead the Identity and Anti-Financial Crime Risk & Devices team.

I write Ruby, Java, and Go depending on what the problem needs. My work sits at the intersection of real-time systems, compliance pipelines, and fraud prevention — the kind of problems where getting it wrong has real consequences.

## Leadership

I've been leading teams for most of my career — not as a manager who drifted away from the code, but as someone who stays hands-on while creating the conditions for others to do their best work.

At PromptCloud, I ran the Ruby on Rails team directly and coordinated the leads of two separate streams — the JobsPikr platform team and the JobsPikr ML team. That meant owning technical direction across disciplines, keeping multiple workstreams aligned, and bridging engineering with product goals.

At Solaris, I now lead a 5-engineer team owning Identity and Anti-Financial Crime — due diligence systems, fraud detection, and device security across millions of customers.

**Solaris Group** — 5-engineer team · Identity & Anti-Financial Crime · Due diligence, fraud detection, device security

**PromptCloud** — Ruby on Rails team · Coordinated leads of JobsPikr Platform & JobsPikr ML teams

## How I got here

I started out building Rails apps for clients at UDProducts — foundational years where I learned to ship, communicate, and own things end to end. Then at PromptCloud, I got to build products from zero: took JobsPikr from an idea to a distributed ElasticSearch pipeline handling **20M daily ingestions** and nearly a million real-time queries. Owning a product — not just a feature — shaped how I think about both engineering and leadership.

Fintech found me through Solaris, and I haven't looked back. There's something genuinely interesting about building in a space where correctness isn't optional.
